Create a passport-style photo for your exam form

Some exam and visa-style forms ask for a passport-style photo with a plain background and your name and date of birth printed below the image. Instead of visiting a photo studio, this tool lets you crop your photo to the correct ratio, adjust brightness and backdrop color, and add your name and date of birth — all in a few clicks.

How to create your passport photo

  1. Upload a clear, front-facing photo, ideally against a plain wall or background.
  2. Adjust the crop box so your face is centered, and use zoom or rotate if needed.
  3. Pick a backdrop color — white, blue, or light gray.
  4. Adjust brightness and contrast if the photo looks too dark or too flat.
  5. Type your full name and date of birth, choose a font and color.
  6. Download as JPG or PNG.

Frequently asked questions

Does this tool remove my background automatically?

No — for best results, take your photo against a plain wall. This tool crops, adjusts brightness/contrast, and adds a colored border and caption around your photo rather than removing a busy background.

Can I change the font size of my name and date of birth?

Yes, use the font size field in the text settings panel to make it larger or smaller before downloading.

Which format should I download — JPG or PNG?

JPG is smaller in file size and accepted by almost every form. Use PNG only if your form specifically asks for it.
