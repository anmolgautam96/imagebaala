Why forms ask for a PDF instead of images

Many admission and exam forms — especially UPSC, CUET, and university applications — ask candidates to upload documents like mark sheets, ID proof, or category certificates as a single PDF instead of separate images. This tool lets you combine multiple photos or scanned pages into one clean PDF file in the exact order your form needs.

How to convert images to PDF

  1. Upload all the images you want to combine.
  2. Drag to reorder pages, rotate any page that is sideways, or delete one you don't need.
  3. Choose your page size (A4, Letter, or Legal) and orientation.
  4. Click "Generate & Preview PDF" and check the result.
  5. Download the final PDF and upload it to your form.

Frequently asked questions

How many images can I combine into one PDF?

There is no fixed limit — you can add as many images as your form requires, each becoming its own page in the PDF.

Which page size should I choose?

A4 is the standard size accepted by almost every Indian government form. Use Letter or Legal only if your form specifically asks for it.

Can I reorder the pages after uploading?

Yes, simply drag any image up or down in the list before generating the PDF.
